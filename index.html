About

Me

I’m Korean. But I was born in Argentina, lived in Mexico and grew up in Los Angeles. Throughout my childhood I have interacted with many different people from different backgrounds. But it was not easy having lived in different places. As a child, I faced discrimination from other children and even adults and people of my own race because I was different. I wanted to be normal. When I grew older, I realized then that being different is not a weakness, it is a gift that defines my identity. I don't have to be the same like everyone else to be accepted because I am who I am. Being able to view certain things from three cultural perspectives is meaningful.

I come from a multi-cultural background, humble beginnings, speak three languages (Spanish, Korean, English), first generation college student, low-income, and undocumented. I am also very shy but once you get to know me I can be a quite quirky.

I approach any and all problems with a design mindset. But the most important question when diving into a problem is to ask why. Why am I doing this, why do people need this, why why. Above all, the reason why I am sharing my story with others? It is crucial that I mirror the diversity of the people I serve in a global manner. I need to understand what the users want, what their lifestyle is like, and put myself in their shoes. That is when I will create a great inclusive product.
